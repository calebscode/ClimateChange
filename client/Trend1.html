Trend details

This trend was derived from two data sets: one documenting yearly global solar power consumption in TWh, and one containing data regarding the average global cost of solar electricity per watt (in USD). In formulating this query, consisting of data from 1985 to 2019, we are able to visually identify a strong positive correlation between the decreasing cost of solar energy and its increasing global consumption (In other words, a strong negative correlation between the raw cost of solar energy and global consumption). This suggests that as solar energy becomes cheaper, more people are choosing this earth-friendly renewable source over more traditional energy sources.
