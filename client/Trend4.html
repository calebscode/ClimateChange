Does the change in electricity access correlate with a change in carbon dioxide consumption?

Default starting year is the earliest year in the database.
Default ending year is the lastest year in the database.

Trend details

Similarly to trends 2 and 3, we again look at global year-over-year CO2 consumption, this time in relation to the yearly percentage of the world population with access to electricity. While a strong correlation wasn't found, there is interesting behavior among these data points. Notice the slight concave down shape. It seems that the yearly increase in CO2 emissions actually peaked around the time that around 80-84% of the population had access to electricity. Beyond that, the year-over-year change in CO2 consumption actually began to decrease. This is interesting because as the world nears 100% access to electricity, we start to see less and less change in CO2 consumption. This is important for beginning to tackle the threat of climate change.
