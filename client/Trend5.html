Trend details

This trend again makes use of two data sets. This time, we look into the relationship between CO2 consumption per capita and consumption of renewable energy per capita. One might expect that as the consumption of renewable energy increases, CO2 consumption would decrease. However, this isnâ€™t strictly what the data tells us. Much like trend 4, we again see a concave down shape in the data. This tells us that the two increased together before finally leveling out and eventually displaying the trend we originally expected. Possible causation for this shape may be rapid increase in population or global access to electricity. Once renewables reached a large enough population, only then did CO2 consumption decrease. This again gives us hope for greater use of renewable energy and decreased consumption of CO2 for the future.
